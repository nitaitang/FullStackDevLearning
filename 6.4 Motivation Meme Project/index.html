Dipteris Conjugata


As often as I’m captivated by the sweeping, umbrella-like fronds of Dipteris conjugata, it’s their young, unfurling leaves—evoking the graceful motion of a jellyfish—that hold a strange, enchanting charm. This quiet corner has become my favorite, a place where you can stand close to them. Elsewhere, they usually cling to the unreachable heights of cliffs or hide along trails only found at higher elevations. I’ve traveled this road three times now, and just when I think I’ve grown tired of it, something always pulls me to pause—and take another photo
